Learning Outcome 01

Integrate the knowledge, theories and methods of a variety of disciplines to inform decision making.

Preventative Teach Project: Patient education is a significant part of a nurse’s responsibility. Education that is integrated with interdisciplinary knowledge and theories empowers patients to improve their health status. This is important because when patients are engaged in their care, they are more likely to incorporate interventions that may increase chances for positive outcomes. During session one, my group and I worked on a preventative teach project where we created a presentation to teach an identified target population about a medical condition. We selected individuals greater than 25 years old as our target population where we educated them about the risks, disease process, and preventative methods of hypertension.

Community Health Lesson Plan: Moving forward from teaching a hypothetical targeted audience, we reached out to our community and did a health teaching lesson in a school-setting. During session four, I worked with a group to create lesson plan to educate high schoolers about nutrition. Our objective was that by the end of the live zoom session, the students will be able to identity the importance of nutrition, food examples of each nutrient discussed, recommended meal portions, and resources available for planning nutritional choices. To achieve these objectives, we included not only evidence-based information from credible sources on our slides, but also inserted various food pictures to enhance visual learning. By then end of the lesson, we designed a Kahoot quiz to reinforce teaching and test our student’s understanding.

Learning Outcome 02

Participate in the process of inquiry, analysis, and information literacy to promote quality care and patient safety.

Health History Project: During our first session, I completed a health history project where I conducted my first-ever interview to inquire a person’s history of past and present illness. This assignment allows me to gain skills in organizational and interview skills, as well as increasing confidence in asking personal questions. It is important to acquire such history taking skills as this is a crucial component of patient care. The first step of the nursing process is assessment, which includes the process of asking about the patient’s chief complaint and taking note of the patient’s past history.

EBP paper: For adult health II course, I wrote an evidence-based paper about acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) based on my experience at the R-ICU. With extensive evidence-based research, I discussed nursing management of the patient’s care and rationale for the nursing interventions in the critical care setting. The following paper details the pathophysiology, nursing management, and QSEN concepts applicable to the care of ARDS patient.

Learning Outcome 03

Apply leadership concepts, abilities, and decision making in the provision of high-quality nursing care, healthcare team coordination, and the oversight and accountability for care delivery in a variety of settings.

Sentinel Event Paper: During the nursing leadership course, I wrote a sentinel event paper to explore unanticipated event in a healthcare setting resulting in death or serious injury to a patient. My sentinel event paper was focused on a case of medication error, where potassium chloride was mistakenly administered through IV push during a code blue. In response to the event, I performed a root-cause analysis on the medication administration error to unveil contributing factors and presented the findings in a fishbone diagram. With use of literature review of similar events, I then designed a corrective action plan that is composed of problem statement, goals and objectives, logic model, budget, and a timeline of work plan. Through writing this paper, I was able to apply a range of leadership skills to evaluate challenges and create evidence-based solutions.

Learning Outcome 05

Demonstrate skills in using patient care technologies, information systems, and communication devices that support safe nursing practice

Technology has become increasingly complex and constantly transforming the way nursing care is delivered. The primary role of informatics in nursing is to prevent errors, increase efficiency, and avoid adverse events. For example, patients may now access their electronic healthcare records online, charting is performed through computer systems, and medication administration involves bar code scanning to ensure patient safety and prevent human error. During my clinical rotation, I gained experience in using Cerner and EPIC.

Other than computerized charting, during my preceptorship at the cardiac telemetry unit of St Joseph’s Medical Center, I had experience of using telemetry to monitor the patient’s cardiac rhythm, respiratory rate (RR), heart rate (HR), and oxygen saturation on a central monitor. Whenever the patient has abnormal rhythms such as atrial fibrillation or HR/RR that are out of range, the central monitor will set off alarms to alert the unit.

Learning Outcome 06

Demonstrate basic knowledge of healthcare policy, finance, and regulatory environments, including local, state, national, and global healthcare trends.

Legislative Paper: For the professional nursing course, I wrote a legislative letter to senator Dianne Feinstein regarding the COVID Community Care Act. This assignment was a valuable opportunity to put what we learned about nurse’s role of political advocacy into practice. In this letter, I urged the senator to cosponsor the bill S. 783 because this bill provides grants to help medically underserved communities to gain better access to healthcare services like diagnostic testing, vaccination, and contact tracing.

Global Health Paper: For the community health course, I collaborated with a group of classmates to focus on a global health issue and develop a thorough plan supported by evidence to suggest probably solution to that public health issue. Our global health paper focused on the issue of guinea worm disease (GWD) in Chad and developed a logic model with an ultimate goal of full eradication by 2026. Through this project, I learned about the contributing factors to the continued presence of GWD, including a lack of general understanding of GWD, dogs as alternative hosts, contaminated water source, and poor surveillance effort. Additionally, I learned about combined effort of regulatory bodies and organizations involved in addressing this health issue. Therefore, we incorporated these organizations as part of the logic model to make realistic and achievable goal within a stated timeline.

Learning Outcome 07

Use inter- and intra-professional communication and collaborative skills to deliver evidence-based, patient centered care.

TeamSTEPPS Presentation: One of the assignments for the professional nursing course was to incorporate what we learned about TeamSTEPPS and apply it in a scenario. TeamSTEPPS is an evidence-based set of teamwork tools with a goal of optimizing patient outcome through improved communication and teamwork among health care professionals. I learned that the key principles of TeamSTEPPS include team structure, leadership, situation monitoring, mutual support, and communication. Our scenario states that a patient with diverticular perforation was sent to the OR. However, the perioperative nurse found that the consent form was not signed and no pre-op protocols were completed. I discussed the importance of two challenge rule and CUS (concerned, uncomfortable, safety issue) rule demonstrated in the scenario. These communication tools may be used to raise one’s concern multiple times to the health professional team, which helps stopping and correcting the situation the safety breach. I am now better equipped with these communication tools and collaborative skills for future conflicts in the healthcare setting.

Learning Outcome 08

Assess health/illness beliefs, values, attitudes, and practices of individuals, families, groups, communities and populations in order to provide comprehensive care to diverse populations.

Cultural Interview: Cultural competence is an important quality because nurses may encounter patients from diversified backgrounds. To learn about a culture other than my own, I conducted a cultural interview on a friend who came from Korea for college. As she was born and raised in Korea, her responses throughout the interview were authentic and gave a great insight of what Korean culture is like. With use of Purnell’s 12 domains as the guide of this interview, I explored different aspects of Korean culture, ranging from family roles and workplace issues to topics like spirituality, death rituals, and health-care practices. I believe this was an enlightening experience because I gained new perspective of other’s culture and understand the importance of respecting people of all backgrounds.

Learning Outcome 09

Demonstrate the professional standards of moral, ethical, and legal conduct.

ANA Code of Ethics Presentation: There is a great emphasis on ethics, morals, and legal conduct in the field of nursing because of the life-and-death situations and ethical dilemmas that nurses may encounter. During the professional nursing course, we worked in groups to closely examine the provision of the ANA Code of Ethics. My group was responsible for provision 2: the nurse’s primary commitment is to the patient, whether an individual, family, group, community, or population. This provision emphasizes patient-centered care by always treating patients with dignity, respect, and compassion. In addition to a presentation, our group created a handout that concludes the main takeaway from provision 2.

Ethical Dilemma Presentation: During the adult health II clinical, my group and I worked on a presentation that helps us learn about ethical dilemmas and evidence-based practice policies. Specifically, we worked on the use of restraints in hospitals. We researched on evidence-based policies regarding restraints from credible sources and compared that with the restraint policy at St. Joseph’s Medical Center. Additionally, we looked into a case study of bedrail asphyxiation due to inappropriate use of restraint and suggested some solutions to prevent similar occurrence.

Learning Outcome 11

Implement holistic, patient-centered care that reflects an understanding of human growth and development, pathophysiology, pharmacology, medical and nursing management across the health/illness continuum, across the lifespan, and in all healthcare settings.

Drug At Bedside Paper: During the pharmacology course, I worked with a partner to write a paper on lithium, which is a mood stabilizer used as a first-line treatment option for bipolar patients. In the paper, we detailed a case study of a bipolar patient that includes his demographics, chief complaint, history of present illness, and the course of his illness. Then, we reviewed the mechanism, pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, side effects, patient teaching, and the cost of lithium. Because I researched extensively on lithium during pharmacology class, this helped me understand why this medication was administered to some of the patients I encountered during mental health clinical. I also understood the importance of closely monitoring the lithium labs and signs of toxicity because of its narrow therapeutic range.
